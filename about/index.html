I will be a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at Luddy School of
Informatics, Computing, and Engineering (SICE), Indiana University Bloomington. I once majored in Bioinformatics at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. My interests range from theoretical aspects of programming languages to philosophy. I am currently exploring Homotopy Type Theory and its computational models (Cubical Type Theory, especially).

I extended A circuit-like notation for lambda calculus to a naive “topological” model of λ-calculus - λ-circuits. Partial evaluation and supercompilation can be shown clearly with them. You can also view them as an alternative approach to natural deduction and Gentzen style reasoning, but they are not eligible to cover dependent types and beyond intuitively. I did this mainly for fun, and out of some minimalistic beliefs.

I spend time reading about German philosophers from Kant to Husserl, and Chinese philosophy like Daoism and Han Buddhism (in Han Chinese only). I have been trying to reconcile rational thoughts on which PL/math/logic are based, and Gemüt on which arts and fundamental interactions between sapiens and universe are based. I have also been trying to bridge different branches of sapiens wisdom.
